## Purpose
Define the initial steps to validate and authorize work, align stakeholders, and create a lightweight plan.

## When to use
Whenever a new project idea or feature proposal is ready to be explored.

## Goals
- Confirm business need and measurable outcome
- Identify stakeholders & champions
- Define success criteria and initial timeline
- Decide go/no-go for planning

## Minimum Deliverables
- Project One-pager (Problem, Goal, Success Metrics)
- Stakeholder list & communication plan
- High-level timeline and key milestones
- Initial risk list
- Resource needs (team roles, rough effort estimate)

## Role Responsibilities During Initiation

### Product Manager
- Defines product vision and business case
- Identifies success metrics and target outcomes
- Secures stakeholder sponsorship and alignment

### Business Analyst
- Gathers initial requirements from stakeholders
- Documents business constraints and dependencies
- Assesses feasibility and business impact

### Project Manager
- Creates project charter and high-level timeline
- Identifies team composition and resource needs
- Establishes communication plan and governance structure

### UX Designer (if applicable)
- Conducts preliminary user research
- Identifies UX constraints and opportunities
- Estimates design effort and timeline

### Stakeholders
- Provide business context and priorities
- Review and approve project charter
- Commit resources and budget

## Initiation Checklist
- [ ] One-pager completed and reviewed by Product Manager
- [ ] Business Analyst assigned and briefed on project scope
- [ ] Initial requirements gathered from stakeholders
- [ ] Sponsor / Stakeholder alignment (email or meeting)
- [ ] Project Manager assigned and charter drafted
- [ ] Team roles identified (Developers, QA, UX, Scrum Master, Release Manager as needed)
- [ ] Communication channels and meeting cadence established
- [ ] Decision: Approve to move into planning?
- [ ] Create repo or project board skeleton
- [ ] Add initial artifacts to repo (docs/ or .copilot/)

## Decision Gate
Move to planning when:
- Success metrics are clear
- Stakeholders agree on priority
- Team availability is confirmed

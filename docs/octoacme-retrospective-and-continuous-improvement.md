# OctoAcme — Retrospective & Continuous Improvement

## Purpose
Capture learnings and convert them into actionable improvements.

## When
After each sprint, release, or important milestone. Also after incidents.

## Structure
- What went well
- What could be improved
- Action items (owner, due date)
- Follow-up on previous action items

## Running a Retrospective
- Timebox: 45–75 minutes depending on team size
- Facilitated by Scrum Master or Project Manager
- Use an anonymous idea board if needed to encourage candor
- Prioritize 2–3 top action items to avoid overload
- Ensure all team voices are heard (Developers, QA, UX, BA, etc.)

## Role Responsibilities During Retrospective

### Scrum Master
- Facilitates retrospective meeting and ensures psychological safety
- Keeps discussion focused and timeboxed
- Helps team identify actionable improvements
- Follows up on previous action items

### All Team Members
- Share honest feedback on what went well and what needs improvement
- Propose specific, actionable improvements
- Volunteer to own action items
- Commit to implementing agreed changes

### Project Manager
- Documents retrospective outcomes and action items
- Adds action items to project backlog with owners and deadlines
- Tracks completion of improvement actions
- Shares lessons learned with other teams/projects

### Product Manager
- Reviews project outcomes against success metrics
- Provides feedback on business impact
- Adjusts future priorities based on learnings

## Tracking Improvements
- Add action items to the project backlog or issues with clear owners and timelines
- Review outstanding actions in the weekly PM sync
